#1: Transportation Fees for Elective Shuttles

Other districts charge for elective-related intercampus travel. If Coppell ISD charged $370/year per rider like Keller ISD, we could raise between $92,500 and $185,000 depending on participation. Fees for all bus users could generate over $2 million annually.

#2: Boost ADA With Better Absence Documentation

Improve how we collect medical or parent notes so absences don't reduce state funding. A 1% improvement in attendance can bring in over $1 million in additional revenue.

#3: Expand Open Enrollment

Frisco ISD projects $750,000 in revenue for every 100 open enrollment students. With targeted marketing and policy updates, New Tech could fill open seats and generate hundreds of thousands in revenue.

#4: Increase Facility Rental Revenue

Other districts use platforms like Facilitron to market and rent out gyms and meeting spaces. CISD could expand after-hours rentals and double facility revenue with better outreach and fee alignment.

#5: Increase and Enforce Technology Fees

Other DFW districts charge $50–$75/year and make tech fees mandatory. CISD charges $35 but doesn't enforce it. Making it mandatory - especially for secondary and choice schools - could raise over $500,000.

#6: Resell or Repurpose Unused Tech Assets

Districts using centralized inventory and surplus resale systems recover 10%–40% of original tech value. Even partial implementation could generate $20,000–$100,000.

Conclusion & Ask

These ideas can generate millions in recurring revenue and avoid disruptive campus closures. We urge the CISD Board to explore and act on these options before making irreversible decisions.
